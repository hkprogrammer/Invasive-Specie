Endangered Specie

Oahu Tree Snails(Hawaiian: Pupu; Scientific: Achatinella)

There are lots of invasive species who are endangering the hawaiian habitat and degrading the environment to make less suitable for Oahu Tree Snail, for example, the strawberry guava who are hostile taking over the Hawaiian native habitats. Or like rats who are impacting the environment of leis and other ornaments. Currently UH Manoa scientist had made a simple breeding program which they have successfully breeded the snails by artificial powers, but now, the government wants hawaii to have three types of protection towards the endangered Oahu Tree Snails.

  1. Protection of all endangered or invaded lands, included the once been tookover by the rats or E. Roseas.
  2. Continue the captive breeding program and re-introduce snails to good habitats.
  3. Preserve , Maintain, and Restore the existing habitats.
